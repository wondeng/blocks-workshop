Lomanthang, Mustang
July 2025

Nepali educator. Block advocate. Workshop facilitator.

I grew up in Lo Manthang, a small walled village in Upper Mustang, Nepal — sitting at 3,840 meters in the Himalayas, just a stone's throw from the Tibetan border. It's one of the most remote places you can imagine, and it shaped everything about how I see children, learning, and the natural world.

I've been living in New York City and teaching at City and Country School for over 14 years. In the summer of 2025, I went back home to Nepal for the first time in seven years. I brought wooden blocks with me and sat with local carpenters who used Himalayan pine to make them by hand. Then I ran workshops for teachers in Mustang — many of whom had never seen open-ended materials like this before. Watching them play with the blocks for the first time, like kids, was one of the best moments of my life.

I also wrote a children's book — illustrated by Lhemi Sherpa. It's a bilingual story in English and Tibetan about a nomadic child in the Himalayas. I wrote it because I wanted children everywhere to see themselves in a book.
